Wenxuan Guo | 郭文轩

 

Welcome to my homepage!

I am a fifth year PhD student in Econometrics and Statistics at the University of Chicago, Booth School of Business, supervised by Tengyuan Liang and Panagiotis (Panos) Toulis. Before joining Booth, I earned my MS in Statistics at UChicago, advised by Lek-Heng Lim. I obtained my BS in Mathematics from Fudan University, advised by Shuqin Zhang.

My research focuses on designing randomized experiments and estimating complex treatment effects in causal inference, primarily using insights from optimization and randomization inference. I also have general interests in hypothesis testing and sequential decision making.
